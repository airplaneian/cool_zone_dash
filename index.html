Airplane Ian's Cool Zone
Situation Report

This report monitors systemic risk across three tightly coupled domains: physical constraints, technology market concentration, and private credit. Based on Richard Bookstaber's framework, it visualizes how vulnerabilities in the physical world can rapidly propagate through interconnected financial structures.

The charts below use live data to provide a real-time look at how these different areas are holding up.

Layer 1: Physical Foundation

Tracks the real-world constraints on the economy. Energy prices serve as a proxy for the cost of physical movement, while semiconductor stocks act as a bellwether for global reliance on hardware supply chains.

Layer 2: Tech Goliaths

Examines how much of the broader stock market's capital is tied up in a handful of megacap technology firms. A widening spread indicates building pressure as investments crowd into the AI infrastructure trade.

Layer 3: Credit Vulnerability

Monitors the cost of capital versus the structural health of non-bank lenders. When treasury yields pull higher but private credit sentiment plummets, it signals that debt markets may be flashing systemic warning signs.
